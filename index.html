Pixxel is a team of students from BITS Pilani, that's participating in the IBM Watson AI XPRIZE competition. Our aim is to satellites faster than any company or government in history.

The Master Plan

What we plan to do.

Collect

Collect high-cadence near real-time satellite imagery and weather data using our nanosatellites.

Analyze

Analyze the data collected using neural networks and cloud computing horsepower.

Predict

Highly accurate forecasts in a multitude of domains. See things before they happen.

Who are we?

Our team consists of over 30 students working in the spheres of data analytics, image processing, astronautical engineering and business development. Our core team mainly consists of members with prior experience from their work at Hyperloop India. 

What is the potential of our idea?

We want to build and launch constellations of ultra compact, highly capable, inexpensive nanosatellites that ‘launch early, launch often’.

The high resolution information from these constellations will enable us to monitor the ever-changing features of the Earth’s surface and emerging situations that influence the environment, the economy and our nation’s security through cutting edge artificial intelligence.

Our work will impact a multitude of verticals such as agriculture, urban civil planning, forestry, maritime monitoring among others.

What do we intend to do?

As a primarily student collaboration, our plan is to leverage our performance in global incentive prize competitions like the IBM Watson AI XPRIZE to bring about a change in how space data is utilized in our country. We believe our work will potentially inspire scientists, engineers and businesses in the country to co-create and innovate in the field of space technology and design.
